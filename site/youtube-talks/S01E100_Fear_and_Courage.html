Fear and Courage

Bhante Bodhidhamma 23:28 YouTube Talks
Source: YouTube

In this insightful talk, Bhante Bodhidhamma examines fear as the most fundamental emotional state dependent on the delusion of self. Drawing from the Dhammapada (verse 39), he explains how the Buddha promised that all fear disappears for one who has awakened, whose mind isn't agitated, and who has abandoned both merit and demerit.

The talk explores six areas where we need courage: physical, social, moral, emotional, intellectual, and spiritual. Bhante emphasizes that fear is actually trying to protect us, so we must "give it a hug" rather than run from it. He shares the Buddha's own experience with fear and terror in the jungle, demonstrating how to turn towards fear and remain present with it until it subsides.

From the courage needed for physical challenges and social activism to the moral courage required in complex ethical situations, Bhante shows how each encounter with fear becomes an opportunity to undermine the delusion of self. The talk culminates with spiritual courage - the willingness to walk the path of renunciation toward nibbāna, despite not knowing what awaits. Through vipassanā practice, we learn to sit with difficult mental states as physical sensations, allowing them to be released naturally rather than creating stories that perpetuate suffering.
