The Ten Fetters

Bhante Bodhidhamma 26:46 YouTube Talks
Source: YouTube

In this illuminating talk, Bhante Bodhidhamma examines the ten fetters (saṃyojana) that keep us bound to saṃsāra, drawing from the Mahāmalunkya Sutta (MN 64). He begins by concluding the discussion on āsavas (mental taints) before exploring how these fetters operate at three levels: underlying tendencies (anusaya), mental obsession, and manifest actions.

The first five lower fetters are: sakkāyadiṭṭhi (identity view), vicikicchā (skeptical doubt), sīlabbataparāmāsa (clinging to rules and rituals), kāmacchanda (sensual craving), and vyāpāda (ill-will). These are abandoned through the first three stages of awakening. The five higher fetters include craving for fine material and immaterial existence (the jhānas), māna (conceit of 'I am'), uddhacca (restlessness), and avijjā (ignorance).

Bhante emphasizes the practical importance of recognizing these fetters in daily life and meditation practice, explaining how different practitioners achieve liberation through either samatha-vipassanā or pure vipassanā approaches. This teaching provides essential understanding for anyone serious about the path to complete liberation from suffering.
