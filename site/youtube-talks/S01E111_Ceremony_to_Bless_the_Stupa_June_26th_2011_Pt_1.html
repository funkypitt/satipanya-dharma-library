This recording captures the opening of a traditional Buddhist blessing ceremony for the stupa at Satipanya Buddhist Retreat. Bhante Bodhidhamma leads the community in sacred Pali chanting, beginning with the traditional homage 'Namo tassa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa' (Homage to the Blessed One, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Awakened One).

The ceremony includes recitation of protective chants from the Pali Canon, including portions of the Ratana Sutta (Jewel Discourse) and the Maṅgala Sutta (Discourse on Blessings). These traditional chants invoke the Triple Gem — Buddha, Dhamma, and Saṅgha — and offer blessings for protection and spiritual welfare. The ceremony follows traditional Theravāda format, moving from the stupa to a reading of the Buddha's first discourse, then to the Mettā Sutta chanting near the Kuan Yin statue.

This first part captures the devotional and communal aspects of Buddhist practice, demonstrating how ritual and ceremony support the cultivation of faith (saddhā) and create conducive conditions for spiritual development. We'll be chanting Namo Tassa, Bhagavato, Arahato, Samma Sambuddhassa. You all with me? Thank you.

When we get to the stupa, the monks at the top will lead us in the three refuges and precepts. Then a reading will be made of the first discourse of the Buddha, and then we'll make our way down to the statue of Kuan Yin where we'll have a reading of the Metta Sutta and a chanting of it. Then we'll make our way back to the marquee where we'll listen to some talks. At the end we'll have a cup of tea.

You'll find all you need for chanting on the back page of this handout. So we'll begin the little ceremony with our chanting.
