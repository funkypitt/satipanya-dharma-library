Misunderstanding re. the Self, Karma and Transcendence

Bhante Bodhidhamma 54:46 Dharma Talks
Source: satipanya.org.uk

In this dharma talk, Bhante Bodhidhamma addresses fundamental questions about self, karma, and transcendence from his explicitly transcendent perspective within Theravāda Buddhism. He clarifies that the Buddha's teaching on anattā (not-self) is not a denial of the conventional self, but rather an inquiry into whether an eternal soul (atta) actually exists as described in Hindu traditions.

The talk explores how awareness gradually separates from identification with the five khandhas through meditation practice, leading to the observer position where one becomes "aware of being aware." Bhante explains how this process can culminate in nibbānic experience, where awareness completely withdraws from the psychophysical organism.

Addressing karma and rebirth, he explains how intentions (cetanā) create karmic consequences through our relationships with pleasant and unpleasant experiences. The discussion includes practical guidance on noting intentions before they become actions, emphasizing this as our only moment of genuine choice. The talk also covers rebirth mechanics, past-life memories, and how saṅkhāras (mental formations) transfer between lives while being purified of their historical content.

This teaching offers profound insights into the transcendent dimension of Buddhist practice, distinguishing it from materialist interpretations while remaining grounded in canonical Theravāda teachings.
