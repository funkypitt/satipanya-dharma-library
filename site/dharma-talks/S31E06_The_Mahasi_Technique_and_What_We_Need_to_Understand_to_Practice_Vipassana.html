The Mahāsi Technique and What We Need to Understand to Practice Vipassanā

Bhante Bodhidhamma 46:43 Dharma Talks
Source: satipanya.org.uk

In this introduction to vipassanā practice, Bhante Bodhidhamma explains the Mahāsi noting technique and the essential understandings needed for insight meditation. He clarifies that techniques themselves don't produce insights—rather, they create conditions for intuitive awareness to function clearly, free from the confusion of identifying awareness with thinking.

The talk covers three key aspects of the Mahāsi method: noting (using simple words to direct attention to present experience), going slowly (to see subtle movements and intentions), and observing intentions (recognizing the moment of choice before actions). Bhante emphasizes how noting helps draw awareness beyond intellectual concepts to direct sensory experience, leading to moments of absorption where the sense of self temporarily disappears.

Drawing from the Buddha's own spiritual journey, he explains right awareness (sammā sati) as coming from 'not-knowing'—the original, clear perception of a child seeing something for the first time. He discusses working skillfully with the five hindrances by neither indulging pleasant states nor pushing away unpleasant ones, but investigating the reactions themselves. The talk concludes with exploring the three characteristics: impermanence (seeing the arising and passing of all phenomena), unsatisfactoriness (understanding how craving creates suffering), and not-self (recognizing the constructed nature of identity). This foundational teaching provides essential guidance for understanding both the practical method and theoretical framework of Mahāsi vipassanā practice.
