How We Use the Self and What Does It Mean for Not-Self

Bhante Bodhidhamma 54:56 International Talks
Source: satipanya.org.uk

In this French-language dharma talk, Bhante Bodhidhamma addresses one of Buddhism's most confusing teachings: how can the Buddha speak of using the self while also teaching not-self (anattā)? Drawing from the Dhammapada and various suttas, he shows how the Buddha never denies our conventional sense of self, but rather encourages us to use it skillfully as a tool for ethical development and spiritual liberation.

The talk explores paṭicca samuppāda (dependent origination) to show exactly when the sense of self arises in our moment-to-moment experience - only after contact, feeling, perception, and craving have already occurred. Bhante discusses the famous dialogue with Vacchagotta, where the Buddha remained silent when asked whether the self exists or not, avoiding both eternalist and annihilationist views.

Using the myth of Narcissus as a teaching metaphor, Bhante illustrates how consciousness becomes enchanted with its own reflections in the mind-heart, leading to identification and delusion. The path to awakening involves stepping back from this enchantment to recognize the 'unborn, unconditioned, and uncreated' - the transcendent reality that makes liberation possible. This talk offers both philosophical depth and practical meditation guidance for understanding one of the Buddha's most subtle teachings.
