Second and Third Noble Truths: Cause and Cessation of Dukkha

Noirin Sheahan 47:12 Noirin's Teachings
Source: satipanya.org.uk

In this teaching, Noirin Sheahan examines the second and third Noble Truths through the lens of paṭicca samuppāda (Dependent Origination). She explains how the twelve links—from avijjā (ignorance) through saṅkhāras (formations) to jarāmaraṇa (aging and death)—create the wheel of dukkha in our lives.

Using accessible gardening analogies, Noirin shows how unwholesome saṅkhāras sprout from ignorance, manifest through our six sense bases, and create the experience of self through upādāna (clinging). She emphasizes vedanā (feeling) as the crucial point where mindfulness can stop the wheel from turning toward suffering.

The teaching beautifully illustrates how we can experience the second Noble Truth—that taṇhā (craving) causes dukkha—and the third Noble Truth—that cessation is possible—in our moment-to-moment meditation practice. Noirin offers practical guidance on cultivating appreciation for absence and space, helping us recognize those quiet moments when dukkha is not present as glimpses of Nibbāna.
